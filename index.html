Indicators

Indicators

These don't have revenue targets yet, but they tell us whether accounts are moving in the right direction. Without tracking them, we risk a leaky bucket where lapsed and churned accounts go unnoticed and graduated accounts never happen.

Graduated Accounts
Positive Indicator
When a self-service account exceeds £500 average monthly profit, it graduates to managed. This is the system working. The platform has grown an account to the point where it's ready for relationship management, already profitable. A high graduation number means self-service is doing exactly what it should be, building real, scalable value and feeding the managed base with accounts that are ready to grow further. A low graduation number is an early warning that self-service accounts are not growing their profit, and the managed pipeline will eventually feel it.
Lapsed Accounts
Warning Indicator
An account that was previously above £500 average monthly profit and has dropped below. This is the leaky bucket. If lapsed accounts go unnoticed, they quietly erode NRR while new business and graduated accounts mask the gap. The NRR and win-back cohorts stay fixed specifically to prevent this. Tracking lapsed accounts means we catch the drop early, rather than finding out at the end of the year that the core base has shrunk underneath the new retailers.
Churned Accounts
Negative Indicator
A churned retailer is one that has gone out of business or actively turned us off. This is permanent revenue loss. Unlike lapsed accounts, there is no recovery path. Every churned retailer reduces the base from which Net Revenue is calculated, making growth harder for every metric. Churn can come from within the managed base, including NRR and win-back accounts, or from self-service. Understanding why retailers churn is as important as knowing that they did.
How It Connects

The Levers Work in Sequence

Every lever protects and grows the £9m. Nothing gets forgotten.
New business brings fresh accounts into the managed base, expanding the pool we can grow from. Win-backs recover 35 specific accounts that have already proven their value, returning lost revenue rather than relying on new retailers to fill the gap. NRR makes sure the core cohort of 188 accounts is actively growing, so the base strengthens rather than just holds steady.

None of these work in isolation. New business without NRR means the core base can quietly erode. NRR without win-backs means recoverable revenue goes unaddressed. Win-backs without new business means the pipeline has no fresh growth. All three have to move together to hit the £9m.

The indicators tell us whether the system is working. Graduated accounts show self-service is feeding managed with profitable, ready partners. Lapsed accounts are an early warning that managed revenue is shrinking. Churned accounts are permanent losses that reduce the pool we are all working to grow.

Managed (£9m) and Self Service (£2m) then combine to reach the £11m Retail Partners target.
