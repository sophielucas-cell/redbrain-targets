Indicators

Indicators

These don't have revenue targets yet, but they tell us whether accounts are moving in the right direction. Without tracking them, we risk a leaky bucket where lapsed accounts go unnoticed and graduated accounts never happen.

Graduated Accounts
Positive Indicator
When a self-service account exceeds £500 average profit per month, it graduates to managed. This is the system working. The platform has grown an account to the point where it's ready for relationship management, already profitable. A high graduation number means self-service is doing exactly what it should be which is building real, scalable value and feeding the managed base with accounts that are ready to grow further.
Lapsed Accounts
Negative Indicator
An account that was previously above £500 average profit per month and has dropped below. This is the leaky bucket. If lapsed accounts go unnoticed, they quietly erode NRR while new business and graduated accounts mask the gap. The NRR cohort (top 188) stays fixed specifically to prevent this. Tracking lapsed accounts means we catch the drop early, rather than finding out at the end of the year that the core base has shrunk underneath the new retailers.
How It Connects

The Levers Work in Sequence

Every lever protects and grows the £9m. Nothing gets forgotten.
To hit £9m Managed, we need all three levers working together. New business brings in fresh accounts, but not at the expense of the existing base. Win-backs target 35 accounts that were performing well in Q4 2025 and dropped off in Q4 2026, making sure they are re-engaged and growing again rather than quietly replaced by new retailers. NRR at 120% keeps the focus on the top 188, making sure the core base grows rather than getting diluted by new business or graduated accounts filling the gap.

The indicators sit between Managed and Self Service because they tell us what is moving and where. Lapsed accounts are a leaky bucket warning. If accounts are dropping below £500 average monthly profit and nobody notices, NRR suffers. Graduated accounts show that automation is working, growing accounts to the point where they are ready for relationship management and feeding the managed base with pre-qualified, profitable partners.

Managed (£9m) and Self Service (£2.2m) then combine to reach the £11.2m Retail Partners target.
